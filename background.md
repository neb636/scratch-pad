# My Background

At my core, I'm a creative — and everything I've done professionally has been in service of that.

I studied Graphic Design, drawn to it because it let me design and build things. But I quickly realized I wanted to go further than what design tools alone could take me. I started teaching myself to code so I could actually build the products and apps I was imagining.

That path led me to a frontend internship at a web company. What started as a mix of design and PHP/WordPress work turned into a deep dive into frontend development — they needed engineering help more than design help, and I leaned in. I was hired on as a frontend developer and never looked back.

Over 12+ years, I've specialized in UI engineering across the full arc of modern frontend: WordPress and jQuery, AngularJS, React, and most recently 7+ years in Angular 2+. I've worked across the stack when needed, but my focus has always been on building functional, polished interfaces with strong user experience. I've pushed back on product owners and designers alike when I felt something was too complex or could serve users better — not out of stubbornness, but because I care about what gets shipped.

## AI as an Accelerant

The same creative instincts that made me effective as a UI engineer are what I bring to building in the AI era. I'm not learning to think differently; I'm finally getting the leverage to execute the way I always wanted to. I see AI as a collaborator — one that lets me move faster, go deeper, and bring ideas to life that would have stayed on the whiteboard before.

As much as I've loved writing JavaScript and TypeScript and hacking together shell scripts to automate my world, I'm leaning into a new set of tools and skills. Building with AI, creating workflows, and yes — writing a lot more Markdown — feels like the natural next chapter. I'm not holding onto the old ways for the sake of it.
